01 — The Thesis
The Record Is Broken
Billions of commerce events happen every day. Almost none of them produce a record that is provably immutable, independently verifiable, or useful to the merchant who generated them.
The Gap

Small Merchants Have No Tools

Enterprise retailers spend $50K+ per year on loss prevention platforms, dedicated LP teams, and analytics infrastructure. The single-location merchant running a Square terminal has nothing — no fraud detection, no case management, no evidence chain. They absorb the loss or they don't find it at all.

The Record

Mutable Data Isn't Evidence

Every transaction record sitting in a traditional database can be altered, deleted, or disputed. When a merchant needs to fire an employee, file an insurance claim, or report theft to law enforcement, they need records that hold up. Today, those records don't exist for SMBs.

The Trust

Intermediaries Own the Truth

Banks, card networks, and POS platforms control the record. Merchants trust that what they see is what happened — but they can't prove it independently. When disputes arise, the merchant is always at a disadvantage because the institution holds the only copy of truth.

The Solution

Capture. Seal. Inscribe.

Real-time webhook capture with zero polling gaps. Write-once PostgreSQL evidence store with INSERT-only enforcement at the database level. Merkle root inscription on Bitcoin for permanent, independently verifiable proof. Three steps. One immutable record.
